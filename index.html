The mind behind the method

Prof. B. D. Tripathi

A scientist who spent fifty years restoring a sacred river — and then turned the same principles toward restoring the human being.

Prof. B. D. Tripathi is a UGC Emeritus Professor and Chairman of the Mahamana Malaviya Ganga Research Centre at Banaras Hindu University, and founder of its Centre for Environmental Science & Technology. He began pioneering research on Ganga river pollution in 1972 — work so significant it was formally recognised by the Parliament of India and helped lay the foundation for the national Ganga Action Plan.

Across 37 years of teaching at BHU, he has published more than 226 research papers in national and international journals, authored 10 books, guided 32 PhD scholars, and developed 8 pollution-control technologies that remain in active use today.

He has served the nation in some of its most important environmental roles: as a founder expert member of the National Ganga River Basin Authority (NGRBA), a member of the State Ganga River Conservation Authority, an expert member of the National Ground Water Advisory Council of the Government of India, and President of the Environmental Conservation Association of India. His autobiography has been published by the National Book Trust, Government of India.

His scientific work spans the physico-chemical health of the river Ganga, the behaviour of air-borne particulate pollution and its mitigation by trees, and the biomonitoring of toxins through plant life. Through decades of this work, one conviction took shape: that the same ecological laws which govern the health of a river — energy flow, adaptation, balance and self-regulation — also govern the health of a human being.

Eco-skills is the distillation of that conviction. It is his quiet second act: a practical, teachable method that lets anyone apply the wisdom of natural systems to their own body and mind, gently and daily.

The method

What is Eco-skills — and why it matters

Eco-skills are life-skills based on ecological principles, designed to improve physical and mental health, self-awareness, concentration, and the development of a whole, balanced personality.

Life on earth began with billions of reactions among air, water and soil in the presence of sunlight. Every cell in your body is still built from those same elements. Eco-skills is the conscious, daily management of the five everyday channels through which they continue to reach you — your breath, the light you receive, the water you drink, the food you eat, and the thoughts you hold.

When pure, oxygen-rich blood circulates freely and the mind rests in a calm, positive state, the body releases its own restorative chemistry — serotonin, dopamine, oxytocin and endorphins. When we live disconnected, rushed and anxious, the body floods instead with stress hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, which over time erode our health, focus and peace. Eco-skills gently tips that balance back toward repair.

Why it matters now. Modern life has quietly separated us from the natural systems that built us. We breathe shallowly, wake to screens instead of sunlight, eat without rhythm, and carry a constant background hum of stress. The result is an epidemic of anxiety, poor sleep, scattered attention, and a sense of disconnection that no single product can fix. Eco-skills does not ask you to escape modern life — it teaches you to re-anchor inside it.

It is scientific, not mystical. The method is built on careful observation of how nature works, translated into simple, repeatable practice. It was developed and refined at the Mahamana Malaviya Ganga Research Centre and standardised using a formal assessment scale (the PMSEH framework) with the help of subject experts and statistical validation.

It is proven. The method was tested on 400 people aged 18–45 across rural, urban and semi-urban areas, measured before practice and again after six months. The shift was dramatic: the number of people in the most unstable, stressed and unfocused category fell sharply, while those in the most stable, self-aware and firm category rose from just a handful to the clear majority.

It is for everyone. Eco-skills is applicable at every stage of life — for students seeking focus and confidence, for working adults managing stress, for teachers and counsellors, and for anyone who senses that the solutions to their challenges might lie not in doing more, but in reconnecting with the nature that made them. The full curriculum is available in both Hindi and English.

The five everyday inputs

💨

Breath

Steady, conscious breathing shifts the nervous system out of alarm and into repair. It is the foundation every other practice is built upon — the fastest way to change your inner state in any moment.

☀️

Light

Morning sunlight resets the circadian clock that governs your sleep, mood, energy and metabolism. Just ten conscious minutes a day can change how you sleep that very night.

💧

Water

Water is the medium of every chemical reaction in your body — including the ones that calm and restore you. Eco-skills treats hydration as a gentle daily rhythm, not a chore.

🌾

Nutrition

What you eat literally becomes your tissues, your hormones and your thoughts. Eco-skills forbids nothing — it asks you to make one input at a time more conscious and better-timed.

🧠

Thought

Sustained, intentional, positive attention is chemical, not naive — it releases the body's own calming hormones. Eco-skills makes that release a daily habit rather than an accident.
